Q: What were the main intellectual goals of the assignment? Please situate these goals in terms of the communication strategies you were to learn or practice.

A: Due to the nature of a podcast project, almost all information is presented with verbal speech, alternating tones, and proper background music. The goal of this assignment is to engage our audience in the discussion of a chosen topic using only audio cues. In the course of making this project, group members have greatly improved their verbal and oral communication.

Q: What is your argument or purpose? How did you make the argument or purpose visible and persuasive in your artifact?

A: In the podcast, our group discussed the severity of unequal representation of minority groups in award shows such as Oscars, and many possible causes contributing to the issue, and some possible means of improvements in the future. To illustrate our argument, we backed every claim using data, presented various actors, actresses, and directors to be our examples, and carefully analyzed the sources found in online journals.

Q: Who is the intended audience for your artifact; why is this an appropriate audience? How is your choice of audience reflected in your artifact?

A: The audience of the artifact apparently consists of all classmates and the professor in the ENGL 1102 class, but we also welcome general people interested in racial problems to hang with us in this 12-minute talk.

Q: What are the defining features of the genre or media that you are using in this project? How do you make use of these features?

A: The main mode of communication used in this podcast project is verbal language and sound effects. Instead of simply connecting prerecorded audios one after another, we enhanced our listeners' experience using soothing background music and conversational style talks thrown between all speakers.

Q: If you had more time for revision, what would you change and why?

A: If our group had more time, we wound further refine the audios in terms of sound quality, topic coherence, and share between different speakers. Had we a chance of making these changes, not only the audience would understand our argument more easily with approval, but also all of our group members would learn more skills and feel most fulfilled.
