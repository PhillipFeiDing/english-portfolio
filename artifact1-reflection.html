Q: What were the main intellectual goals of the assignment? Please situate these goals in terms of the communication strategies you were to learn or practice.

A: While the most obviously straightforward goal of the book trailer is to produce high-quality trailer video under applicable means, under the course's specific context we also set goals for ourselves in terms of WOVEN communication mode: in the process of producing this integrated artifact, we honed our skills of filming, acting, and editing, which count for the oral, nonverbal and electronic components of communication.

Q: What is your argument or purpose? How did you make the argument or purpose visible and persuasive in your artifact?

A: On one hand, the ultimate purpose of the trailer in terms of audience lies in enticing them to read the story we work with. To capture their attention, we need a trailer filled with suspenseful things, building tensions, and thought-provoking themes. The selection, ordering, and special effects all contributed to our purpose of displaying the conflicts of ongoing thoughts of the magistrate, from whose perspective the story unfolds part of itself for the readers to explore more in it.

Q: Who is the intended audience for your artifact; why is this an appropriate audience? How is your choice of audience reflected in your artifact?

A: Our potential audience includes, but not limited to young college students and consumers who enjoy reading or viewing political allegories. Due to the nature of the story, we used some scenes to strengthen the visual effects and more accurately represent the true conditions in the story to the particular group audience who may experience resonance with the story and thus are willing to read the story.

Q: What are the defining features of the genre or media that you are using in this project? How do you make use of these features?

A: Though the general media of the project is video, it is more than a mere combination of visual and audio information. The 3-minute trailer consists of numerous clips that integrate into a coherent story stream and hint a particular theme we intend to convey. The use of conversations, blank dark scenes, voice-overs, and silence all increase its suspenseful tone.

Q: If you had more time for revision, what would you change and why?

A: If we had more time to revise this project. We would certainly have more time for shooting all scenes for best quality, and possibly place out other scenes with weaker purposes. More time devoted to the editing phase would also ensure a more coherent, integrated, and visually compelling final product.
