Q: What were the main intellectual goals of the assignment? Please situate these goals in terms of the communication strategies you were to learn or practice.

A: Similar to the first-week video assignment in ENGL 1101, this assignment expects students like us to identify a particular WOVEN mode most struggled with and set up a general goal for refining skills related to the mode in the new course. For example, in terms of electronic communication, I expect to:

* sharpen both my techniques of using media producing applications such as iMovie and also

* collaboration skills essential for consistent teamwork to better engage audiences.

Q: What is your argument or purpose? How did you make the argument or purpose visible and persuasive in your artifact?

A: The argument I made in this assignment is that to better my electronic communication skills, I need to understand the elements of synchronized teamwork and the importance of revision. For persuasion, I used the following:

* specific examples from my ENGL 1101 experiences with video making projects and

* a blueprint of my expectations in the new class.

Q: Who is the intended audience for your artifact; why is this an appropriate audience? How is your choice of audience reflected in your artifact?

A: The general audience includes but is not limited to my English professor and all GT students interested in electronic communication and eager to improve their skills through a variety of approaches.

I hope my video also identifies similar issues that my peers experience, so they can gain a different perspective on particularly how to confront these challenges. To target my audience, I incorporated my past experience of working in a group and the problems we had.

Q: What are the defining features of the genre or media that you are using in this project? How do you make use of these features?

A: The most apparent genre used in the project is video, but it is also a symphony of clips, still images, and sound effects. I used iMovie for editing purpose.

Utilizing basic transitions and visual layout designs, I connected live video clips and simple animation made for illustration of abstract ideas. I also employed sound enhancement technologies to make my words more audible.

Q: If you had more time for revision, what would you change and why?

A: I would probably make some revisions to my script for it to sound more like a verbal conversation rather than a prewritten recitative statement, as I do feel my tone sounds a bit unnatural.

I would also smoothen up my transitions and use of piled-up images. I also wonder if the use of delightful background music can create a different mood for the project.
