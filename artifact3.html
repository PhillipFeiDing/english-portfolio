Introduction

        The personal vignette project offers students an opportunity to showcase their creative writing abilities. By focusing on a particular character, event, or scene, the student should use their picturesque expressions and descriptive languages to express the world around him/her. The main purpose of a vignette does not lie in what exactly happened as the overall plot, but rather on the impressionist effect it conveys through the carefully crafted scenes and imageries by the author. For this particular assignment, the student is expected to write three independent vignettes that contribute to a consistent theme of his/her own choice ranging from “transitions, loneliness, mentors, loss, secrets, procrastinations, and faith … there are many possibilities”.

        When I initially encountered this assignment, I chose to identify my theme first to guide my brainstorming process. I found my lives have dramatically changed in the recent few years, 
and the first pieces of memories that flashed to my mind are of the experiences as an exchange student when I arrived in America for the first time. The year I stayed in Arizona was like a thrilling adventure and meaningful transition to me even from my current perspective. Among the numerous memory fragments popped out in my brain, I zoomed into a few fuzzy ones that collectively contribute to a consistent theme: the transition of personal beliefs and values. I would like to write about how I gained my first baking experience, working experience, and social experience of dancing with a partner. Underlying the explicit theme of transitions is the implicit implication of cultural difference and identity confusion, which recur as cues scattered in the passages for readers to find out. The ordering of the vignettes is not simply chronological, but instead follows the order in a dimension that is closely tied to the theme: how I made my step-by-step changes toward openness and acceptance of my identity as well as adaptation to new cultural values. By completing this project, not only had I practiced writing skills unique from the conventional essay writing but also I gained a deeper understanding of my own identity and values as an exchange student studying in America.
