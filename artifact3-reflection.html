Q: What were the main intellectual goals of the assignment? Please situate these goals in terms of the communication strategies you were to learn or practice.

A: The goal of this assignment is to emulate the writing style of Sandra Cisneros while reflecting on and writing about one's own experiences and moments. Overall, the vignettes should integrate to a unifying theme, However, different from conventional academic essays, the project particularly needs to include:

* sharp imageries and descriptive languages and

* impressionistic scenes and moments.

Q: What is your argument or purpose? How did you make the argument or purpose visible and persuasive in your artifact?

A: Though a vignette does not explicitly state the purpose of the piece in the beginning or conclusion part, it still reflects the theme by selectively picking scenes and creating particular imageries. For this project, I chose the following as the theme:

* specific examples from my ENGL 1101 experiences with video making projects and

* a blueprint of my expectations in the new class.

Q: Who is the intended audience for your artifact; why is this an appropriate audience? How is your choice of audience reflected in your artifact?

A: The immediate audience to the assignment includes my ENGL 1102 classmates and Dr. Hwang who will be reading and reviewing my drafts. However, I also would like to recommend readers of all ages who experience similar personal and emotional struggles when they face a new culture and have to adapt to it.

Q: What are the defining features of the genre or media that you are using in this project? How do you make use of these features?

A: Despite being majorly a writing assignment, the vignette writing style is very different from conventional essay production. This poses a brand-new challenge for me in that I need to include specificity, imageries, and creative languages that contribute to the overall theme implicitly rather than stating it plainly. I captured specific scenes that can potentially include these elements and particularly focused on them when I reviewed my writing.

Q: If you had more time for revision, what would you change and why?

A: Some parts of my vignette project still read like an essay, and they lack some deep-level specificity, or the specificity they create does not align with the overall theme. I would pay special attention to some of these parts if given an opportunity to further revising my draft.
