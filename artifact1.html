 Introduction

        In this assignment, our group carefully read Waiting for the Barbarians by J.M. Coetzee and created a 3 to 4-minute trailer regarding the content of the book to entice future readers to take a look at this wonderful piece. After the completion of this assignment, we not only familiarized ourselves with the main characters and the themes in the book, but also practiced several modes of communication to capture the audienceâ€™s attention, which includes but not limited to electronic, verbal, and non-verbal communication.
        As the first collaborative project in my ENGL 1102 experience, the book trailer our group produced for Waiting for the Barbarians met most of our expectations with exceptional success. The procedure we followed in making this video project effectively divided the originally tremendous work into considerably more perceivable, manageable, enjoyable subtasks that evenly optimized each of our group members' contribution. Our idea of the book trailer originated from one of the themes of the book related to the mental confusion and strangle experienced by the protagonist magistrate, so we initially decided to have this character run through our entire trailer. Meanwhile, after a holistic review of the book and lively discussion within the group, we unanimously agreed to establish a suspenseful story-telling tone for the trailer. We carefully analyzed each potential scene selected from the book and evaluated their effectiveness for achieving our goal to best engage our audience, in the process by which we came up with our blueprint: the storyboard. Because of our use of the storyboard as our guideline, the actual filming process went more efficient than we had imagined, in that each member was clearly aware of their scenes, lines, and actions. During the early editing phase, while arranging the order of clips and cutting unnecessary scenes, I also gained a deeper understanding of the book theme, which in turn facilitated our decision on organizing our final production.
