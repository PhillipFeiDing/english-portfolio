 Introduction

        Today, podcasts have become a powerful media tool for people to discuss a variety of topics ranging from personal anecdotes to societal problems. In Artifact II, the students are expected to create an 8- to 12-minute long podcast discussing on one of the approved topics, including but not limited to globalization, multiculturalism, multilingualism, and diversity. Students will enjoy the full freedom of utilizing and combining only sound elements to achieve a symphony of thoughts, moods, and tones regarding their topic.


      Among the various available topics for composing Artifact 2, our group focused on race and diversity issues nowadays. Specifically, we discussed these issues on the part of award shows like Oscar and examined the cause of apparently unequal representation of colored people and women winning such awards because we believe actors/actresses of different heritages in award shows provide the most powerful illustration of the issue. After conducting basic research on the topic, we oriented the research toward a few subtopics around the issue that we plan to include in our podcast: how exactly we should define this issue related to racial diversity using strong facts and meaningful data, and whether there are alternative explanations to the issue rather than concluding a charge on racism. As a result, we discovered the issue is more complicated than most people assume. It turns out many factors, decisions of producers, tastes of audiences, and even the values possessed by different cultures, all have some influences on the representation of minority groups in the award shows. Instead of fixing every word to say with a full script, our group listed all potential topics to cover and organized our podcast in a conversational style. Therefore, most parts of the project sound like friends' normal talking and free flow of a diverse range of ideas. In the editing phase, appropriate background music and sound enhancements were employed to optimize our audiences' experience.
